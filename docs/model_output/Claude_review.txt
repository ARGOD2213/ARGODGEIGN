SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE VERDICT
================================================================================

VERDICT: CONDITIONAL GO

Confidence: HIGH (Conservative assessment per Claude model profile)
Date of Review: 2026
Platform Stage Assessed: Post v1 IoT Alert Engine, Pre Industrial AI Platform

WHY — 5 BULLETS:

  1. SAFETY ARCHITECTURE IS INCOMPLETE [PSE + CA]
     The rule-based engine supremacy principle is stated but not
     architecturally enforced. There is no documented SIL assessment
     (IEC 61511 Clause 9) for any safety-critical function. Until
     SIL ratings are assigned to compressor trip logic, urea HP
     section overpressure, and NH3 leak detection, go-live at a
     100-acre ammonia-urea complex is not defensible to PESO or OISD.

  2. SENSOR COVERAGE HAS CRITICAL GAPS [RE + PSE]
     22 sensor types are built. A minimum viable set for the 5
     declared critical assets (compressors, reformer burners, urea HP,
     boiler feed, instrument air) requires approximately 38-42 distinct
     measurement types by API 670 and IEC 61511 requirements. Reformer
     burner tube metal temperature and urea HP section corrosion
     monitoring are absent entirely.

  3. HUMAN RISK MODEL IS LEGALLY VALID BUT TECHNICALLY INCOMPLETE [OH]
     The competency + fitness + exposure + fatigue model is sound and
     avoids discriminatory logic. However, it has no biological exposure
     tracking (NH3 TWA/STEL per ACGIH TLV), no heat stress index
     computation (WBGT per ISO 7933), and no permit-to-work integration.
     These are enforceable under Factories Act 1948, Schedule 8.

  4. DATA ARCHITECTURE WILL NOT SCALE AT SENSOR VELOCITY [IDA]
     DynamoDB single-table design for iot-sensor-events with no defined
     partition key strategy will produce hot partitions at 50+ machines
     x 22 sensors x 1Hz = ~1,100 writes/second sustained. DynamoDB
     limit per partition is 1,000 WCU. This is a production outage
     waiting to happen on go-live day.

  5. COMPLIANCE TRACKING IS A DASHBOARD ONLY — NOT AN AUDIT SYSTEM [CA]
     The compliance model tracks status. It does not generate the
     evidentiary artifacts required for PESO inspection, factory
     inspector audit under Factories Act, or environmental compliance
     under EP Act 1986. A dashboard that shows GREEN does not satisfy
     a statutory inspector requiring Form 7 (accident register),
     Form 21 (annual return), or environmental monitoring logs.

CONDITIONS FOR GO (must be met before Phase 1 production):
  [ ] SIL assessment documented for minimum 3 safety functions
  [ ] DynamoDB partition key strategy redesigned for sensor velocity
  [ ] Alarm rationalization applied to SQS consumer (EEMUA 191)
  [ ] NH3 biological exposure tracking added to human safety model
  [ ] Statutory artifact generation added to compliance module

SECTION 3: MACHINE REALISM MATRIX
================================================================================

FORMAT: Machine Class | Min Sensors Required | Detectable Faults |
        Not Reliably Detectable | False Positive Risks | Mitigation
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. CENTRIFUGAL COMPRESSOR (NH3 Synthesis, Refrigeration)
   Min Sensors : Vibration (radial x2, axial x1), bearing temp (x4),
                 lube oil pressure, seal gas pressure, suction/discharge
                 pressure, suction/discharge temp, flow (suction),
                 shaft speed (API 670 mandatory set)
   Detectable  : Imbalance (1x vibration), misalignment (2x), bearing
                 wear (broadband RMS rise), seal failure (seal gas loss),
                 lube oil failure (temp rise + pressure drop), surge onset
                 (flow + pressure oscillation)
   Undetectable: Impeller fouling (no flow curve monitoring currently),
                 internal erosion, labyrinth seal wear (indirect only),
                 motor winding fault (MCSA not implemented)
   FP Risks    : Vibration spike on process upset (not mechanical fault),
                 bearing temp rise on hot ambient day (not bearing fault)
   Mitigation  : Process-correlated vibration filtering; ambient temp
                 compensation on bearing temp thresholds

2. REFORMER BURNERS (Primary/Secondary Reformer)
   Min Sensors : Tube metal temp (pyrometer, 4 zones min), flue gas
                 O2/CO analyser, fuel gas pressure, fuel gas flow,
                 combustion air flow, stack draft, flame detector (UV/IR),
                 skin temp uniformity index
   Detectable  : Flame failure (UV/IR), fuel overpressure, O2 deviation
                 (air-fuel ratio), tube hot spot (pyrometer)
   Undetectable: Tube carburisation (lab analysis only), refractory
                 hot spot behind tubes, catalyst bed channelling
   FP Risks    : Pyrometer reading error from soot on lens,
                 UV detector false trigger from adjacent flame
   Mitigation  : Pyrometer auto-clean cycle; UV detector cross-check
                 with IR; tube temp trend (not single point) for alarm

3. UREA HP SECTION (Reactor, HP Stripper, HP Condenser, HP Scrubber)
   Min Sensors : Operating pressure (x3 per vessel, 2oo3 voting),
                 temperature (x2 per vessel), CO2/NH3 flow ratio,
                 corrosion coupons (ultrasonic thickness, 6 points),
                 carbamate pump stroke counter, relief valve position
   Detectable  : Overpressure (pressure transmitter), temperature
                 excursion, carbamate pump failure (stroke loss),
                 relief valve lift (position indicator)
   Undetectable: Stress corrosion cracking (SCC) initiation,
                 weld defect propagation, liner disbonding
   FP Risks    : Pressure spike from control valve hunting (not leak),
                 temperature rise from feed composition change
   Mitigation  : 2oo3 pressure voting to confirm alarm;
                 corrosion rate trending over 30-day window;
                 AE (acoustic emission) for SCC — FUTURE phase

4. BOILER FEED WATER SYSTEM (BFW Pumps + Deaerator)
   Min Sensors : BFW pump: vibration, bearing temp, suction/discharge
                 pressure, flow, motor current; Deaerator: level, temp,
                 pressure, dissolved O2 analyser
   Detectable  : Pump cavitation (suction pressure + pulsation),
                 bearing fault (vibration), motor overload (current),
                 deaerator O2 breakthrough (analyser)
   Undetectable: Pump internal erosion (indirect via efficiency calc),
                 deaerator tray fouling
   FP Risks    : Vibration spike on BFW system startup (normal transient),
                 dissolved O2 analyser drift
   Mitigation  : Startup suppression window (5 min post-start);
                 O2 analyser calibration check weekly

5. INSTRUMENT AIR SYSTEM (Compressors + Dryers + Distribution)
   Min Sensors : Air compressor: vibration, bearing temp, discharge
                 pressure, discharge temp, interstage temp;
                 Dryer: dew point analyser, switchover timer;
                 Distribution header: pressure at 5 key take-off points
   Detectable  : Compressor fault (vibration), air dryer failure
                 (dew point rise), distribution loss (header pressure)
   Undetectable: Gradual valve seat leak in distribution (diffuse),
                 moisture carryover into instrument tubing
   FP Risks    : Dew point spike on high ambient humidity days,
                 pressure dip on high instrument air demand (startup)
   Mitigation  : Ambient humidity compensation for dew point alarm;
                 demand-correlated pressure threshold

6. AMMONIA SYNTHESIS LOOP (Converter + Heat Exchangers + Separator)
   Min Sensors : Converter inlet/outlet temp (multi-point, 12 min),
                 converter pressure, recycle gas H2/N2 ratio analyser,
                 inert gas purge flow, NH3 separator level + temp,
                 catalyst bed temp profile (4 beds x 4 points)
   Detectable  : Catalyst hotspot (bed temp), H2/N2 ratio deviation,
                 separator high level (carryover risk)
   Undetectable: Catalyst poisoning (slow — only via conversion
                 efficiency decline), converter tube cracking
   FP Risks    : Catalyst bed temp variation from feed gas fluctuation
                 (not catalyst damage)
   Mitigation  : Feed-corrected catalyst efficiency KPI; temp trend
                 rate-of-change alert (not absolute value only)

7. COOLING WATER SYSTEM (Cooling Towers + Circulation Pumps)
   Min Sensors : Supply/return temperature, flow, conductivity, pH,
                 tower fan motor current + vibration, blowdown valve
                 position, makeup water flow
   Detectable  : Fouling onset (rising supply temp), pump fault
                 (vibration), chemistry deviation (conductivity, pH)
   Undetectable: Legionella growth (lab analysis only), internal
                 scale distribution
   FP Risks    : High supply temp on hot summer day (not fouling)
   Mitigation  : Ambient wet bulb temperature correction on supply
                 temp alarm; seasonal threshold adjustment

8. ELECTRICAL SUBSTATION (HV/LV Transformers + Switchgear)
   Min Sensors : Transformer oil temp, winding temp, buchholz relay,
                 oil level, LV bus voltage, power factor, harmonic
                 distortion analyser
   Detectable  : Oil overheating, winding fault (buchholz), voltage
                 deviation, high harmonic distortion
   Undetectable: Insulation degradation (partial discharge — requires
                 PD sensor not in current set), turn-to-turn fault
   FP Risks    : Oil temp alarm on high load + high ambient (normal)
   Mitigation  : Load-corrected transformer temp threshold;
                 add partial discharge sensor in Phase 2

SECTION 4: CASCADE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

FORMAT: Initiating Failure | Immediate Effect | Secondary Effect |
        Plant-Wide Effect | Est. Production Loss
------------------------------------------------------------------------

CASCADE 1: NH3 SYNTHESIS COMPRESSOR TRIP
  Initiating  : High-high vibration on NH3 synthesis gas compressor
                (bearing failure, rotor imbalance, or surge)
  Immediate   : Compressor trip. Synthesis loop pressure drops.
                NH3 synthesis stops within 2-3 minutes.
  Secondary   : NH3 refrigeration compressor loses load (synthesis
                gas supply cut). Refrigeration system unstable.
                Urea plant loses NH3 feed. Urea synthesis stops.
  Plant-Wide  : Full integrated complex shutdown. Reformer continues
                producing syngas — must reduce or shut to match.
                Boiler load imbalance. Possible torch flaring.
  Prod. Loss  : 100% urea production. ~$80,000-150,000 USD per day
                (ASSUMPTION based on 2,000 MT/day urea plant average).
                Restart time: 12-36 hours minimum.
  AI Response : Rule engine: CRITICAL alert via SNS within 30 seconds.
                LLM: Context analysis of trend leading to trip.
                Dashboard: Cascade propagation map auto-activates.

CASCADE 2: REFORMER TUBE FAILURE
  Initiating  : Tube metal temp >930 deg C sustained OR tube rupture
  Immediate   : H2 + CO release inside reformer box. Emergency fuel
                shutoff triggers (SIS). Reformer trips.
  Secondary   : Synthesis gas supply to NH3 loop cut. NH3 loop
                coasts down on inventory (15-30 min). After that,
                NH3 synthesis stops. Urea plant stops.
  Plant-Wide  : Full plant shutdown. Steam system surplus —
                dump to condenser. Boilers may need to reduce firing.
  Prod. Loss  : 100% production. Restart: 5-15 days (tube inspection,
                refractory repair, relight procedure).
                Estimated loss: $400,000 - $2,250,000 USD.
  AI Response : Rule engine: CRITICAL (reformer_tube_temp > 920 deg C).
                LLM: NOT authorised to modify SIS action.
                Dashboard: Reformer zone highlighted. Evacuation
                          protocol triggered for reformer area.

CASCADE 3: UREA HP SECTION OVERPRESSURE EVENT
  Initiating  : HP reactor pressure rises above 185 bar (design
                175 bar). Relief valve lifts OR catastrophic failure.
  Immediate   : NH3 + CO2 + urea solution release. Toxic cloud.
                Possible explosion if ignition source present.
  Secondary   : Plant-wide emergency shutdown (ESD). All personnel
                evacuation. Emergency services called.
  Plant-Wide  : 100% plant shutdown. Likely regulatory investigation.
                Possible production license suspension.
  Prod. Loss  : Minimum 30-day shutdown for investigation + repair.
                Loss: $2.4M - $4.5M USD + regulatory fines.
  AI Response : Rule engine: CRITICAL — directly trigger ESD signal
                advisory to control room (operator confirms).
                LLM: BLOCKED — no analysis during active ESD event.
                     Analysis only permitted post-event in review mode.

CASCADE 4: BOILER FEED WATER PUMP FAILURE
  Initiating  : BFW pump cavitation or bearing failure. Pump trips.
                Backup pump fails to start (single point of failure).
  Immediate   : Boiler drum level drops. Boiler low-low level trip.
  Secondary   : Steam supply to reformer drops. Reformer H2O:C ratio
                falls. Carbon deposition on reformer catalyst begins.
  Plant-Wide  : Reformer emergency shutdown to protect catalyst.
                NH3 synthesis stops. Urea synthesis stops.
  Prod. Loss  : 100% production. Catalyst damage possible.
                Restart: 24-72 hours. Catalyst replacement: 2-4 weeks.
  AI Response : Rule engine: WARNING on pump vibration/cavitation onset.
                CRITICAL on boiler low-low level.
                LLM: Recommend BFW pump maintenance during next
                     planned shutdown window.

CASCADE 5: INSTRUMENT AIR FAILURE
  Initiating  : Main instrument air compressor failure + standby not
                started. Header pressure drops below 5 bar(g).
  Immediate   : Pneumatic control valves fail to position (fail-safe
                open or closed per design). Process upsets cascade.
  Secondary   : 30-50 control loops simultaneously go to manual or
                fail-safe. Operators overwhelmed. Multiple trips likely.
  Plant-Wide  : Full plant trip within 5-10 minutes of IA failure
                (typical fertilizer plant operating experience).
  Prod. Loss  : 100% production. Restart: 6-24 hours.
  AI Response : Rule engine: P1 alert on IA header pressure < 6 bar(g).
                CRITICAL on < 5 bar(g) with standby not running.
                LLM: NOT called during IA failure — system may be
                     degraded. Rule engine operates standalone.

SECTION 5: HUMAN SAFETY + LEGAL CHECK
================================================================================

VALID CONTROLS:

  1. Competency-based risk scoring
     Valid under: Factories Act 1948 Section 7A (occupier obligations),
     ISO 45001:2018 Clause 7.2 (competence)
     Implementation: Competency matrix per role + task. Score
     decreases with time since last training/certification.

  2. Fitness-for-duty model (non-discriminatory)
     Valid under: Factories Act 1948 Section 45 (first aid + medical),
     ISO 45001:2018 Clause 8.1
     Implementation: Shift medical check status (binary: FIT/UNFIT).
     Basis: shift medical exam — not age, gender, or ethnicity.

  3. Exposure tracking by zone and duration
     Valid under: Factories Act 1948 Schedule 8 (toxic substances),
     MSIHC Rules 1989 Clause 4 (information disclosure)
     Implementation: Zone entry/exit logging with ambient concentration.

  4. Fatigue scoring based on shift hours
     Valid under: Factories Act 1948 Section 54 (hours of work),
     Section 56 (overtime limit: max 50 hrs/quarter, 60 hrs/week)

INVALID / RISKY CONTROLS — DO NOT IMPLEMENT:

  1. AGE-BASED risk scoring
     Risk: Violates Equal Remuneration Act 1976, Article 14 Indian
     Constitution (equality before law). Creates grounds for
     discrimination lawsuit.
     Replace with: Task-specific physical fitness criteria only.

  2. Medical history-based scoring (unless disclosed voluntarily)
     Risk: Violates privacy under Personal Data Protection framework
     (India DPDP Act 2023). Employee cannot be scored on undisclosed
     medical conditions.
     Replace with: Current-shift fitness check only (binary).

  3. Automated removal from duty based solely on AI risk score
     Risk: AI decision to remove a worker = employment action.
     Requires human supervisor approval under Indian labour law.
     Replace with: AI flags risk score > threshold -> Supervisor
     review required -> Supervisor confirms/overrides removal.

  4. GPS tracking of individual workers without consent
     Risk: DPDP Act 2023 requires consent for location tracking.
     Replace with: Zone-level entry/exit logging (badge/RFID),
     not continuous GPS tracking.

LEGALLY SAFER ALTERNATIVE FRAMEWORK:

  Human Safety Risk Score = f(
    competency_gap_score [0-25 pts],
    shift_fatigue_score  [0-25 pts — FAID-lite model],
    current_exposure_pct [0-25 pts — % of TWA consumed],
    task_hazard_level    [0-25 pts — based on PTW type]
  )
  Total = 0-100.

  Thresholds:
    0-50   : Normal duty. No action.
    51-70  : Supervisor notification. Worker informed.
    71-85  : Supervisor review REQUIRED before task assignment.
    86-100 : Restricted duty. Supervisor MUST confirm.

  All scoring logic documented and auditable.
  All threshold changes require Safety Manager sign-off.
  Workers can view their own scores at any time (DPDP compliance).

FORMAT: # | KPI Name | Formula | Data Source | Alert Thresholds

--- MACHINE HEALTH KPIs ---

KPI 01: OVERALL EQUIPMENT EFFECTIVENESS (OEE)
  Formula   : OEE = Availability% x Performance% x Quality%
              Availability = (Planned time - Downtime) / Planned time

  Source    : DynamoDB iot-sensor-events + production data

  Formula   : MTTR = Total downtime hours / Number of repair events
  Source    : Work order system (PTW open to close time)
  Thresholds: GREEN < 4h | YELLOW 4-8h | RED > 8h (critical machines)

KPI 04: VIBRATION SEVERITY INDEX — per rotating machine
  Formula   : VSI = RMS_vibration_current / RMS_vibration_baseline

  Source    : Vibration sensor tags in DynamoDB

KILL RISK 1 — SAFETY EVENT BEFORE SIL ASSESSMENT IS COMPLETE
  Scenario  : Platform is deployed to a real ammonia plant. A CRITICAL
              alert fires on NH3 synthesis compressor. LLM analysis is
              ambiguous. Operator delays response. NH3 release occurs.
              Investigation reveals: platform had no SIL assessment,
              no documented LOPA, no IEC 61511 compliance.
  Result    : Criminal liability under Factories Act Section 92.
              PESO suspends facility. Platform banned from industrial use.
              Developer and company face prosecution.
  Prevention: IEC 61511 SIL assessment for 3 safety functions BEFORE
              any real plant deployment. Non-negotiable.

KILL RISK 2 — ALARM FLOOD ON FIRST DAY OF PRODUCTION
  Scenario  : Platform connected to real plant data. Without alarm
              rationalization, 1,100 sensors fire simultaneously on
              startup. SQS backlog grows. SNS floods operator phones.
              Operators silence all alerts within 2 hours.
              Platform is never used again.
  Prevention: EEMUA 191 alarm rationalization implemented and tested
              with Lambda failure simulation BEFORE production.
              Target rate: max 6 actionable alarms per operator per hour.

KILL RISK 3 — DYNAMODB COST OVERRUN IN WEEK 1
  Scenario  : All 50 machines x 22 sensors at 1Hz connected on go-live.
              DynamoDB on-demand billing: $40-50/day. AWS bill at end
              of month: $1,200-1,500. Project budget ($140/6 months)
              exhausted in 3 days.
  Prevention: Adaptive sampling strategy implemented from Day 1.
              AWS Budgets alert set at $20/month. Auto-stop EC2 at limit.

KILL RISK 4 — LLM GIVES WRONG ANALYSIS DURING ACTIVE NH3 EVENT
  Scenario  : NH3 leak in synthesis area. LLM called with incomplete
              context (calibration not done, sensor data BAD quality).
              LLM returns: "Normal fluctuation, no action needed."
              Rule engine returns: CRITICAL.
              Dashboard shows both. Operator reads LLM output.
              Evacuation delayed by 8 minutes.
  Prevention: Rule engine severity ALWAYS shown as primary alert.
              LLM analysis shown in secondary "AI Insight" panel only.
              LLM analysis tagged with data quality + confidence score.
              During CRITICAL events: LLM panel suppressed. Rule engine only.

KILL RISK 5 — SPONSOR REJECTS AT DELIVERY — NO DEFINED DONE CRITERIA
  Scenario  : After 6 months of build, sponsor is shown the platform.
              Sponsor says: "I expected real-time control recommendations,
              not just dashboards." Or: "I need it integrated with our
              existing SCADA, not a standalone system."
              No written scope agreement exists.
              Project declared failed. Time and cost written off.
  Prevention: One-page scope statement signed before Phase 1 code.
              Must define: deliverables, acceptance criteria, integration
              scope, what is NOT in scope, and demo conditions.

KILL RISK 6 — CYBERSECURITY BREACH VIA SPRING BOOT TO OT PATH
  Scenario  : Spring Boot application (internet-facing or on shared IT
              network) is compromised. Attacker discovers direct network
              path to plant historian or OPC-UA server. Sends malformed
              data or commands to OT layer. Plant trips or worse.
  Prevention: Zero write path from Spring Boot to OT. Architecture
              documented and verified. IEC 62443 zone separation.
              Penetration test before any plant-site deployment.

KILL RISK 7 — WORKER SAFETY LEGAL ACTION FROM FLAWED RISK SCORING
  Scenario  : Human safety risk score incorrectly scores a worker as
              HIGH RISK based on faulty sensor data or undocumented

              Investigation finds: scoring logic undocumented, no appeal
              process, no transparency to worker.
  Prevention: All scoring logic documented. Worker can view own score.
              No automated removal — supervisor always confirms.
              DPDP Act compliance: consent, transparency, right to
              contest.

KILL RISK 8 — PLATFORM UNAVAILABLE DURING CRITICAL SAFETY EVENT
  Scenario  : EC2 t3.micro is in STOPPED state (cost saving via
