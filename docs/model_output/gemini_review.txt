1) Executive Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The architecture is theoretically sound but practically fragile. While the dashboarding hierarchy is logical, the current design underestimates the interdependency of chemical process variables and mechanical health. You have a "silo" risk: treating a compressor as a standalone mechanical asset rather than a component of a pressurized gas loop. Approval is granted only if the AI feedback loop includes Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) verification for every synthesized insight before it reaches the control room.

5) Human Safety Legality Check
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Non-Compliant: Using "Age" or "Gender" as a fatigue/fitness variable (Violates Indian Constitution Art. 15 & Global DEI).
* Valid: Using "Time Since Last Rest" and "Ambient Heat Index Exposure" (IS 14489:1998).
* Safer Framework: Shift to Predictive Exposure Limit (PEL). If the AI detects a worker has been in the "Ammonia Pump House" (High Temp/Noise) for >4 hours, the Dashboard issues a "Mandatory Rotation" alert.

12) What can kill this project in production
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The "Crying Wolf" Effect: High false-alarm rates in vibration analytics leading operators to mute the dashboard.
2. Instrumentation Drift: AI making decisions based on uncalibrated +/- 5% pressure transmitters.
3. Cyber-Physical Breach: A compromised AI dashboard being used to mask a real process excursion.
4. Operational Ego: Senior operators ignoring AI insights because "they've run this plant for 30 years without a computer telling them how."
